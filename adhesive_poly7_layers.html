Adhesive Shear-Lag Teaching App, Polymer 7-Sublayer Version

Updated version: top metal / adhesive 1 / seven polymer sublayers / adhesive 2 / bottom metal. The bottom metal strain is imposed uniformly and acts as the driving layer. Width b is retained as an input for reference, but is currently unused because this formulation is per unit width.

1. Model used in this updated version

What this model assumes
This is a one-dimensional axial shear-lag model along the bonded length. It is intended for trend interpretation, not as a full 2D or 3D continuum solution through thickness. The bottom metal layer is prescribed to deform at a uniform axial strain. The top metal and the seven polymer sublayers are not directly gripped and acquire strain through the upper adhesive, the internal polymer shear couplings, and the lower adhesive.

In this updated version:
  • the bottom metal strain is imposed and constant
  • the top metal strain is solved
  • the polymer is split into seven equal sublayers, each with its own solved displacement and strain field
  • the upper and lower adhesives carry different shear-stress distributions
  • the polymer transfers load internally by shear between adjacent sublayers
  • the solution is obtained numerically along the bonded length
  • width b is shown in the input panel but is not used in the present per-unit-width formulation
Imposed bottom-metal strain: ε0 = ΔLs / Ls
Prescribed bottom-metal displacement: ub(x) = ε0 x
Top metal equilibrium: Et tt utt'' = k1 (ut - up1), with k1 = Ga1 / ta1
Polymer sublayer i equilibrium: Ep·tsp·upi'' = (upi-1 − upi) + (upi+1 − upi) for interior layers; at top: (ut − up1) + (up2 − up1); at bottom: (ub − up7) + (up6 − up7) ; at the two ends of the polymer stack, coupling to adhesive 1 or adhesive 2
Internal polymer coupling between adjacent sublayers: kp = Gp / (tp / 7) = 7 Gp / tp
Upper adhesive shear: τ1 = k1 (ut - up1), lower adhesive shear: τ2 = k2 (up7 - ub), with k2 = Ga2 / ta2
Internal polymer shear between adjacent sublayers: τp,i,i+1 = kp (upi - upi+1)

The charts use the bonded-strip coordinate only, from -L/2 to +L/2. The imposed lower metal strain is shown as a constant reference line. All Plotly legends are interactive, so individual curves can be switched on or off. For the polymer families, only the top and bottom polymer sublayers are visible by default to avoid overcrowding.

4. Automatic model checks

These checks focus on physically relevant quantities for this 1D model: end strain, not end displacement, is used to assess the free-end approximation in the solved layers. Additional checks report centre shear symmetry and a consistency estimate for Ep and Gp under an isotropic linear-elastic interpretation.

5. Key outputs

6. Chart 1: local strain in all solid layers

The bottom metal is the imposed driver and remains flat at ε0. By default only the top and bottom polymer sublayers are shown. Use the legend to show or hide any individual line.

7. Chart 2: axial normal stress in the solid layers

The top and polymer stresses are solved fields. The bottom-metal stress is shown as the imposed strain multiplied by Eb, because its strain is prescribed in this kinematic driving model.

8. Chart 3: adhesive and internal polymer shear stresses

For symmetric loading, the adhesive and internal polymer shear curves should pass through zero at the centre. By default only the first and last internal polymer shears are shown among the polymer interfaces.

9. Chart 4: displacement of the solved solid layers

The vertical gaps between adjacent displacement curves are the slips or internal mismatch displacements that generate shear transfer.

10. Chart 5: interfacial and internal slips

Slip is the relative displacement across the upper adhesive, the internal polymer interfaces, and the lower adhesive. By default only the first and last internal polymer slips are shown among the polymer interfaces.

11. Chart 6: normalised strain transfer

A value of 1 means the local strain equals the imposed bottom-metal strain ε0. Comparing the seven polymer sublayers shows how much of the through-thickness gradient remains at each x-position.

13. Expandable theory and verification notes

Assumptions
  1. The bottom metal is directly driven and has a prescribed uniform strain ε0.
  2. The top metal and the seven polymer sublayers are not directly gripped.
  3. Both adhesives are thin and transmit shear only through a linear elastic relation τ = G δ / t.
  4. The polymer transfers axial load internally through linear elastic internal shear couplings.
  5. Small strain and linear elastic axial response are assumed for the solved solid layers.
  6. The model is one-dimensional along the bonded length. It does not resolve full 2D or 3D through-thickness continuum behaviour.
  7. Width b is retained for reference but is currently unused because the present equations are written per unit width.
Material consistency note for Ep and Gp
For an isotropic linear-elastic material, Young's modulus and shear modulus are related by G = E / [2(1 + ν)]. This app allows Ep and Gp to be entered independently because they may be treated as effective values. The automatic checks estimate the implied Poisson's ratio ν = Ep / (2 Gp) - 1 and warn if it is outside the isotropic linear-elastic range.
Useful limiting cases to test yourself
  • Increase Gp strongly: the seven polymer sublayers should collapse towards one common response.
  • Decrease Gp strongly: the lower polymer sublayers should follow the imposed driver much more closely than the upper ones.
  • Increase Ga2 strongly: the bottom polymer sublayer should follow the bottom metal more closely.
  • Increase Ga1 strongly: the top metal should follow the upper polymer sublayer more closely.
  • Set ΔLs = 0: all fields should collapse to zero.
Important note on bottom-metal properties
Because the bottom-metal strain is prescribed, its thickness does not enter the solved transfer equations. Eb is used only if you wish to display the implied bottom-metal stress σb = Eb ε0.
