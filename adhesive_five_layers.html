Adhesive Shear-Lag Teaching App, Model 2

Five-layer teaching version: top metal / adhesive 1 / plastic / adhesive 2 / bottom metal. The bottom metal strain is imposed uniformly and acts as the driving layer.

1. Model used in this five-layer version

What this model assumes
The bottom metal layer is prescribed to deform at a uniform axial strain. The top metal and the plastic core are not directly gripped and acquire strain through two adhesive shear-transfer paths.

In this corrected five-layer version:
  • the bottom metal strain is imposed and constant
  • the plastic strain is solved, not assumed uniform
  • the top metal strain is solved
  • the two adhesive layers carry different shear-stress distributions
  • the solution contains two transfer modes, not one
Imposed bottom-metal strain: ε0 = ΔLs / Ls
Prescribed bottom-metal displacement: ub(x) = ε0 x
Top metal equilibrium: Et tt utt = k1 (ut - up), with k1 = Ga1 / ta1
Plastic equilibrium: Ep tp upp = k1 (up - ut) + k2 (up - ub), with k2 = Ga2 / ta2
Upper adhesive shear: τ1 = k1 (ut - up), lower adhesive shear: τ2 = k2 (up - ub)
Two transfer eigenvalues: r± = [(a+b+c) ± √((a+b+c)² - 4ac)] / 2, with a = k1/(Et tt), b = k1/(Ep tp), c = k2/(Ep tp)

The charts use the bonded-strip coordinate only, from -L/2 to +L/2. The imposed lower metal strain is shown as a constant reference line.

4. Automatic model checks

These checks help verify that the five-layer solution behaves sensibly: top and plastic end strains should be near zero, both adhesive shear stresses should pass through zero at the centre, and the bottom-metal strain should remain equal to the imposed value everywhere.

5. Key outputs

6. Chart 1: local strain in all solid layers

The bottom metal is the imposed driver and remains flat at ε0. The plastic and top metal respond through two adhesive transfer paths, so each generally follows a two-mode hyperbolic profile.

7. Chart 2: axial normal stress in the solid layers

The top and plastic stresses are solved fields. The bottom-metal stress is shown as the imposed strain multiplied by Eb, because its strain is prescribed in this kinematic driving model.

8. Chart 3: adhesive shear stresses

The upper and lower adhesive layers can carry very different shear levels. Both curves should be antisymmetric and pass through zero at the centre for symmetric loading.

9. Chart 4: displacement of the three solid layers

The vertical gaps between the displacement curves are the adhesive slips. The gap between top metal and plastic belongs to adhesive 1, while the gap between plastic and bottom metal belongs to adhesive 2.

10. Chart 5: interfacial slips

Slip is the relative displacement across each adhesive layer. In this sign convention, upper slip is ut - up and lower slip is up - ub.

11. Chart 6: normalised strain transfer

A value of 1 means the local strain equals the imposed bottom-metal strain ε0. The top metal typically lags the most, while the plastic usually sits between the two metals.

13. Expandable theory and verification notes

Assumptions
  1. The bottom metal is directly driven and has a prescribed uniform strain ε0.
  2. The top metal and plastic are not directly gripped.
  3. Both adhesives are thin and transmit shear only through a linear elastic relation τ = G δ / t.
  4. Small strain and linear elastic axial response are assumed for the solved solid layers.
  5. The model is one-dimensional along the bonded length.
Why there are now two transfer lengths
In the earlier three-layer case there was one transfer route and therefore one transfer parameter. Here the plastic core is coupled upward and downward, so the coupled differential system has two eigenmodes. That is why the solution involves λ+ and λ− rather than a single λ.
Useful limiting cases to test yourself
  • Increase Ga2 strongly: the plastic should follow the bottom metal more closely.
  • Increase Ga1 strongly: the top metal should follow the plastic more closely.
  • Make the plastic very soft: most strain drop will occur before the top layer.
  • Set ΔLs = 0: all fields should collapse to zero.
  • Make L very short: transfer should remain incomplete everywhere.
Important note on bottom-metal properties
Because the bottom-metal strain is prescribed, its thickness does not enter the closed-form transfer solution. Eb is used only if you wish to display the implied bottom-metal stress σb = Eb ε0.
